Random Badass Whitewater Kayaking Factoids

Always, I mean always, respect the river gods! Poo hits the fan if you don't. I mean it. And never think you've 'beat' the river and not gotten your beat down in a hole or swam before you get off the river for the day...or you're inviting such things into your day before it's all over. And if you do swim, the booty beers or whatever else is concocted are disgusting...so just don't swim! You're safer in your kayaking anyways. Just sayin'.

Always set up safety kayakers below a hairy section of water, whether it be a waterfall or just big, tricky water. Safety kayakers can help in a rescue situation, or if you swim they can help catch your boat, paddle and any other gear that you are departed from.

"Chundered" is a termed used for when a kayaker gets caught in a whitewater hole or some other river feature that holds them and often that rolls them over and the kayaker has to keep trying to not only get and stay upright, but get out of the holding water feature. Getting chundered is never fun and is very exhausting. Some occasions require outside assistance from other boaters (your safety boaters or anyone at some point) by way of throwing you a safety rope and you hopefully being pulled free along with your gear.

When You End Up Swimming

Yep, it's inevitable, especially in the beginning, that you will swim. Even once you are a seasoned kayaker, you will have your moments of swimming. The amount of time between swims just becomes a longer. At these times there is a way to handle being in the water. Yes, this is going to be a run-on paragraph too... When you find yourself swimming you want to be sure your feet are downstream, your hindend is lifted toward the surface of the water to avoid any unseen rocks, boulders, logs, or other unseen harm, and keep ahold of your paddle and kayak as best you can unless you have other kayakers who tell you they will take them for you and they have a hand on the piece of gear you are to let go of. I'm a bad one for letting go of my gear. It's expensive and while it often gets in my way as I try to see what's coming up downstream, I have a hard time letting it go, even when others say to.
